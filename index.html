About Kelsey Fuller

Hi, I'm Kelsey Fuller — and I believe travel helps you live life to the fullest.

(yes, the pun is intended — and yes, I fully own it)

My Story

My love affair with travel started after college, when I packed my bags and moved to Chile with nothing but curiosity and a willingness to figure it out as I went. I spent months working in hostels and bed and breakfasts across the country — and somewhere along the way, something shifted.

I met people from every corner of the world, heard stories that expanded my understanding of what life could look like, and felt my eyes open to possibilities I hadn't even known existed. I came home a different person. And I've been chasing that feeling ever since.

That experience also planted a seed professionally. I spent the next decade building a career in hospitality — specifically in hotels — because I loved making people feel welcome. I loved the human connection. I loved being part of someone's experience of somewhere new. For ten years, that was enough.

Then the company I was working for went bankrupt. And as disorienting as that was, it turned out to be one of the best things that ever happened to me — because it forced me to ask a question I'd been too busy to ask:

```
"What do I actually love most about this work?"

The answer was clear. It was never the buildings or the brands. It was the people.

The Trips That Changed Me

I've taken a lot of trips in my life. But a few have genuinely changed me.

The first was Chile — that post-college leap into the unknown that taught me what I was made of and showed me how wide the world really is.

The second was Morocco. I went as a solo traveler on a group trip hosted by a travel influencer I'd followed for years. It was shortly after a significant chapter of my life had closed, and I needed something to crack me back open. Morocco delivered. The people I met, the places we explored, the conversations we had — it was soul-nourishing in the truest sense. That trip showed me firsthand what it feels like to show up somewhere alone and leave feeling like you belong somewhere new.

The third was Australia. I'd always dreamed of diving the Great Barrier Reef, so I booked a week-long liveaboard and made it happen. Afterward I traveled through Queensland before landing in Melbourne — a city that felt, inexplicably and immediately, like home. I made incredible connections there and fell completely in love with the place. Australia holds a very special piece of my heart, and I'll be back.

Why Likeminded

Today, alongside Likeminded Group Travel, I'm also an Airbnb host — and hosting has taught me something that years in hotel management never quite could. When you're able to create a truly personal experience for someone, when you know their name and their story and what would make their stay feel special — that's when hospitality becomes something magical.

I started Likeminded Group Travel because I believe something deeply: we are all so much more alike than we are different. We just need the right conditions to find that common ground. Travel creates those conditions. It strips away the noise of everyday life, puts us somewhere unfamiliar, and suddenly we're open — to new experiences, new ideas, new people, and new parts of ourselves.

My job is to help you find those experiences. To connect you with trips and people that expand your world. And to take care of every detail in between so you can show up fully present and ready for whatever the journey brings.

Because life is meant to be lived fully. And I'd love to help you do exactly that.
```

Why I Started Likeminded Group Travel — And Why Travel Has Always Been My North Star

A note from Kelsey Fuller, Founder of Likeminded Group Travel

```

There's a moment I keep coming back to.

I'm standing on the deck of a liveaboard dive boat somewhere in the Coral Sea, the Great Barrier Reef stretching out beneath me in every direction. The water is impossibly blue. The world is completely quiet except for the sound of waves. And I remember thinking — this is it. This is what life is supposed to feel like.

I'd booked that trip alone. Flown to Australia alone. Showed up to that boat knowing nobody. And somewhere between the first dive and the last sunset, I felt more like myself than I had in years.

That trip didn't just check a bucket list item. It cracked something open in me. And it's a big part of why Likeminded Group Travel exists today.

It Started Long Before Australia

My love of travel goes all the way back to a post-college decision that probably seemed a little crazy to the people around me at the time. Instead of settling into a traditional career path, I packed my bags and moved to Chile. I spent months working in hostels and bed and breakfasts across the country — immersing myself in local life, learning the rhythms of a place completely different from anything I'd known, and meeting people from every corner of the world.

It was my first real solo travel experience. And it changed everything. I came home with a completely different understanding of the world — and of myself. Travel had shown me that the world was so much bigger and more beautiful than I'd realized — and that I was capable of navigating it on my own terms.

Ten Years in Hospitality — And What It Taught Me

After Chile, I built a career in hospitality. Hotels, specifically — nearly a decade of learning what it means to make people feel truly welcome. To anticipate what someone needs before they even know they need it. To create an environment where a stranger walks in and immediately feels at home.

I loved that work. But if I'm honest, there was always something missing in the corporate hotel world. The scale of it meant that guests were often numbers on a spreadsheet rather than humans with stories. The personal touch — the part I loved most — got lost in the logistics.

Then the company I was working for went bankrupt. And as destabilizing as that was, it gave me something unexpected: a moment of stillness. A forced pause in which I had to ask myself a question I'd been too busy to sit with.

What do I actually love most about this work?

The answer came quickly and clearly. It was never the buildings. It was the people.

The Trip That Showed Me What Was Possible

Around the same time, I found myself at a significant crossroads in my personal life. I needed something. I just wasn't sure what. Then I saw it — an Instagram travel influencer I'd followed for years was hosting a group trip to Morocco. Something about it called to me in a way I couldn't quite explain. I didn't know anyone going. I just knew, in that quiet certain way you sometimes know things, that I needed to be on that trip.

So I booked it.

What happened in Morocco I can only describe as soul-nourishing. The colors, the chaos, the kindness of strangers, the magic of a place so completely unlike anything I'd ever experienced. But more than the destination — it was the people. A group of strangers who showed up individually and became, over the course of just a few days, something that felt remarkably like community.

I came home with a renewed sense of self. With friendships I still cherish. And with absolute clarity about something I'd been circling for years: this is what travel can do. And I want to help other people experience exactly this.

And Then There Was Australia

If Morocco showed me what was possible in a group, Australia showed me what was possible alone. I'd always dreamed of diving the Great Barrier Reef. So I booked a week-long liveaboard, flew to Queensland, and made that dream a reality.

After the liveaboard I traveled through Queensland before making my way to Melbourne — a city I'd never visited and had no particular expectations of. What I found there was one of the most unexpectedly beautiful experiences of my life. I fell in love with the city almost immediately. I made incredible connections. I felt, in a way that's hard to articulate, completely at home.

Australia holds a very special piece of my heart. And I have every intention of going back.

Why Likeminded Group Travel

All of these experiences — Chile, Morocco, Australia, a decade in hospitality, years of creating personal experiences for guests as an Airbnb host — led me here.

I started Likeminded Group Travel because I believe travel is one of the most powerful tools we have for becoming more fully ourselves. It gets us out of our routines and our comfort zones. It shows us how many different ways there are to live. And perhaps most importantly — it connects us with other human beings in a way that strips away the superficial and gets straight to what matters.

I believe we are all so much more alike than we are different. We just have to create the conditions to find that common ground. Travel — the right kind of travel, with the right people, in the right places — creates those conditions beautifully.

That's what Likeminded Group Travel is here to do. Because life is meant to be lived fully. And I can't wait to help you do exactly that.

— Kelsey Fuller, Founder of Likeminded Group Travel
```
